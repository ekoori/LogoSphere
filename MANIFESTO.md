# The LogoSphere Manifesto

**LogoSphere** — a sphere of shared meaning, reason, and value.

The name comes from *logos*: word, reason, meaning. Where a marketplace measures worth in price, LogoSphere measures it in meaning — the values an act serves, the trust it builds, the reasons it matters. This is a platform for a gift economy governed by the people who live in it.

## What we believe

- **Giving is the root transaction.** Goods, skills, and time can move between people without money and without a ledger of debts. What flows back is trust, not payment.
- **Meaning is the real currency.** Every act carries the values it serves. We make those values explicit so that people and communities can find each other through what they care about.
- **Decisions belong to the people they affect.** Liquid democracy puts governance in the hands of contributors, with delegation that can flex as trust and expertise shift.
- **Transparency is the precondition for trust.** Interactions and decisions are visible by default, so reputation is earned in the open.

## The vocabulary

LogoSphere speaks deliberately. The words below are the canonical terms across the product, the code, and these docs.

| Term | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **LogoSphere** | The platform — a sphere of shared meaning, reason, and value. |
| **Meaning Graph** | A living map of the values, receipts, spheres, and relationships of a profile, alliance, or project. |
| **Meaning Receipt** / **Receipt** | An endorsed record of what happened and why it mattered. |
| **Meaning Trail** | The living trace of receipts over time. |
| **Acknowledgement** | Lightweight appreciation — less formal than a receipt. |
| **Sphere** | A community or context with shared norms and meaning. |
| **Alliance** | A federation or partnership between people or spheres. |
| **Openings** | Where possibilities for contribution are posted. |
| **Need** | Something a person or group is asking for. |
| **Offer** | Something a person or group can contribute. |
| **Interaction** | The real human event that happens between people. |
| **Governance / Decisions** | How a community decides — delegation, voting, and policy. |

Some words we keep because they already carry the right meaning: **Sphere**, **Offer**, **Profile**, **Project**.

Some words we retired because they narrowed the idea: a *marketplace* implies buying; a *transaction* implies exchange; a *union* implies a single shape of organising. Not every interaction is an exchange, and not every alliance is a union — the language should hold the whole gift economy, not just the part that looks like trade.

## In practice

- A neighbour rebuilds a community garden fence. That's an **Interaction**. The thanks it earns, recorded and endorsed, is a **Receipt**. A public note of appreciation is an **Acknowledgement**. Together, over time, they form that neighbour's **Meaning Trail**.
- A repair collective and a seed-keeping group federate to share tools and knowledge. That's an **Alliance**, nested inside a **Sphere** of shared values.
- Someone offering GPU time, or asking for hands on a Saturday build, posts to **Openings** — an **Offer** or a **Need**, each tagged with the values it serves so the **Meaning Graph** can match them.

This is not a rewrite of how people already give. It's a language for naming it.
